# Coach Migration Guide — Instructors and Head Coaches

This guide walks you through everything that changed in your day-to-day workflow now that we have moved from Spark Membership to the new WordPress Gym system. Each section follows a simple pattern: what you used to do in Spark, and what you do now.

---

## 1. Your Daily Hub

**Old (Spark):** Log into Spark Membership. Check the attendance tab. Navigate to rank management. Bounce between sections to piece together what is happening today.

**New:** Log into WordPress and land on the **Gym Dashboard**. Everything you need is on one screen.

- **Left panel:** The Coaching Agent — your AI assistant (more on this below).
- **Right side widgets:** Today's attendance by location, promotion-eligible students, your classes today, and program member counts.
- **Menu:** Simplified to **Gym** and **Profile**. Head Coaches also see **Users**.

You no longer need to hunt through multiple Spark tabs. The dashboard surfaces what matters right now.

---

## 2. Checking Attendance

**Old (Spark):** Open the check-in tab or rely on the member app. Limited filtering and no trend data.

**New:** Go to **Gym > Attendance**. You get three tabs:

- **Today** — A live view of today's classes with check-in counts. Each class is listed with its time, number enrolled, and number checked in. Use this during or after class to verify everyone is marked.
- **History** — A searchable table of all attendance records. Filter by date range, class, student, or location. Use this when you need to look up a specific student's record or pull data for a conversation.
- **Trends** — Week-over-week attendance stats. At-risk alerts flag students whose attendance is dropping. This is your early warning system for identifying who needs a check-in conversation before they disappear.

---

## 3. Manual Check-In

**Old (Spark):** Open check-in, search a name, confirm.

**New:** Two options:

1. On the **Attendance > Today** tab, find your class and click **Check In** next to the student's name.
2. Use the **Quick Check-In** form at the top of the page — search by name, select the class, done.

The front desk kiosk handles self-service check-in automatically via QR scan or name search, so most students will already be checked in before you look. Manual check-in is for the ones who slip through.

---

## 4. Viewing Student Progress

**Old (Spark):** Open the member profile, find the rank section, manually count classes and days to figure out where they stand.

**New:** Two options:

1. **Ask the Coaching Agent:** Type "Show me [student name]'s progress." You get an instant response with current belt and stripes, total classes, classes since last promotion, days at current rank, current streak, badges earned, and Foundations status.
2. **User profile:** Navigate to the student's WordPress user profile for the same data in a structured view.

No more manual counting. The system tracks everything automatically.

---

## 5. Belt Promotions

**Old (Spark):** Go to rank management. Manually check if a student has enough days and classes. Update the rank by hand. Hope you did not miss anyone.

**New:** Go to **Gym > Promotions**. The system auto-calculates eligibility based on configurable thresholds (minimum days at rank plus minimum classes). Each student row shows:

- Name, current belt, attendance count, days at rank, and eligibility status.

Your workflow depends on your role:

- **Recommend (all coaches):** Click **Recommend** to flag a student for Darby's final review. Add your assessment notes — what you have observed in class, readiness, areas of strength.
- **Promote (Head Coaches):** Click **Promote**, select the new rank (stripe or belt), and confirm. The system handles everything downstream: updates the rank record, sends an SMS notification, awards the appropriate badge, and publishes a celebration post.
- **Bulk actions:** Select multiple students with checkboxes for batch promote or batch recommend.

The key difference: you no longer need to manually check whether a student has enough days or classes. The dashboard does the math and surfaces who is ready.

## 6. Foundations Program (New)

This did not exist in Spark. Foundations is a structured safety program for new Adult BJJ students. It has three phases:

- **Phase 1:** 10 classes of coached instruction.
- **Phase 2:** 2 supervised rolls with coaches.
- **Phase 3:** 25 total classes completed.

When a new Adult BJJ student is enrolled, they enter Foundations automatically. Here is what you need to know:

- **Recording a supervised roll:** Ask the Coaching Agent "Record a coach roll for [student name]." This logs the supervised roll toward their Phase 2 requirement.
- **Clearing a student:** When all phases are complete, the student is marked "ready to clear." A coach with clearance permission marks them as cleared for live training.
- **Checking status:** Foundations status appears on the student's user profile and is included whenever the Coaching Agent reports on a student's progress.

## 7. Pre-Class Briefings (New)

This did not exist in Spark. Before each class, ask the Coaching Agent:

> "Give me a briefing for tonight's 6pm BJJ class."

The agent returns:

- Enrolled students for that session
- Who is returning from an absence
- Students nearing milestones (50th class, streak milestone)
- Promotion candidates in this class
- Any current announcements

Use this to personalize your coaching. Welcome back students who have been absent. Acknowledge milestones on the mat. Plan your technique focus around who is in the room.

---

## 8. Announcements

**Old (Spark):** Word of mouth, maybe an email through Spark.

**New:** Go to **Gym > Announcements**. You can view all current announcements from admin — schedule changes, events, closures, and anything else the team has posted.

Head Coaches can create announcements: click **Add New**, write the message, assign it to a location (or both locations), and publish. All coaches and front desk staff will see it on their dashboard.

---

## 9. Class Schedule

**Old (Spark):** Navigate to the schedule section in Spark.

**New:** Go to **Gym > Classes**. Each class shows its name, day, time, instructor, capacity, and status. You can view and reference the schedule here. Most editing is handled by Darby and Amanda. Head Coaches can create and edit classes directly.

---

## 10. Coaching AI Agent (New)

This is entirely new. The Coaching Agent is an always-available assistant that knows your students, your schedule, and your program data. It lives in the left panel of your dashboard.

Things you can ask:

- "Who is eligible for promotion at Rockford?"
- "Show me attendance trends for the Tuesday evening BJJ class."
- "Which students have missed more than 2 weeks?"
- "Help me assess [student name] for [next rank]." — The agent reviews their attendance, time at rank, and any notes, then gives you an assessment you can use as a starting point.
- "What should I focus on for today's class?" — Returns a briefing with full student context.

All data is real-time from the gym system. Read queries (looking up students, checking trends) are instant. Actions like "flag for promotion" or "record coach roll" go through the approval queue so nothing happens without the right sign-off.

---

## 11. Daily and Weekly Checklists

### Daily

- Check the **Gym Dashboard** for today's widgets and alerts.
- Review **Attendance > Today** before or after your classes.
- Verify all students are checked in.
- Ask the Coaching Agent for a class briefing before each session.
- Note any students who seem ready for promotion.

### Weekly

- Review **Attendance > Trends** for your location.
- Check the **Promotions** dashboard for new eligibility.
- Submit coach recommendations for promotion-ready students.
- Follow up with at-risk students flagged in Trends.
